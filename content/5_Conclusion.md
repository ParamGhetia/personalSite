Capitalism kills the conditions that make great design possible and destroys it if it survives anyway. 

As Berger made clear, what we know and believe shapes our “ways of seeing.” Capitalism has made sure that what we know and believe about design and what of it we interact with is as little and safe as possible. We have been conditioned into acceptance of the impoverished visual world it produces. By doing so, human imagination and perceptual capacity has narrowed.

“We are homo aestheticus, the art-created art creators.”(Dissanayake 21) We owe it to ourselves to demand better.
